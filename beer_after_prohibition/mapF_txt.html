I finally started talking to some real beer drinkers. It seems that none of them can imagine drinking a gallon of beer. Instead they want to "get a feel" for the relative amount of drinking going on, by seeing units of pints. A quick back-of-the-napkin calculation states that since there's 8 pints in a gallon and 52 weeks in year, I can easily display my values in pints. Of couse that's too easy, I have to make this harder for myself...

Design Decision

Start by adding a nice label and my first actual, d3 image - the pint. I placed one in each region and then displayed the values next to it. Though it was nice having an image it wasn't enough, I wanted to scale it.

I made a rookie mistake thinking that I could start with a base value and just display the width and height as a multiple of that value. For example, the width woulud be 1.5 * the base amount. This threw my images into chaos as they were definitely not to scale. Here's the right way to do it. Calculate the area first and then take the square root of that number. Now you can ensure the correct height and width values. We also have a pretty graphic that seems to move on the page as you run through the animation.
