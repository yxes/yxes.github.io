I was fully statisfied with my creation of course, but others weren't feeling the love. Complaints ran the gamut of the legend taking up too much space while being too close to the data, to the axis being too thick and covering up the points in the first year.

Design Decision

I squeezed the legend and moved it to the far left so it wouldn't conflict with the data. I also reordered it to match the relative lines of the data. This made it much easier to look up the Northeast not only by it's color but with location. After pairing down the axis lines to a more manageble level, I made them slightly opaque to allow the points to show through. Since this is a time series, it made more sense to link our points with lines. I was certain this would appease the masses.

VIEW

If you look closely, you'll see a problem with the layout that I was unable to fix. In SVG the order in which you create an element determines the z-index (front <-> back) of the display. Though I was creating the axis early on in my code, the points still fall behind the axis.
