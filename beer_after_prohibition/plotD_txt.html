Now everyone agrees that this is a good looking graph. Except that they can't really tell exactly what the values are. In other words does that high point in 1945 really mean 26 or is it greater than that? As we move further to the right it's even harder to tell which number it really is...

No matter how much explanation I go into about how this is how a scatter (or maybe line now) plot is supposed to look, I finally caved...

Design Decision

The left axis really needed help, but I was afraid that the plot would become too "busy". I tried sketching out how the plot would look if I copied the labels to each side, but that detracts from the concept that the timeline has a definite starting point and goes on from there. It doesn't solve the problem of how to reference the amounts. Another option was to just to remove the labels on the left altogether. That didn't go over well either. In the end, I had the labels extend as horizontal lines for reference points. I kept them dashed to fade them into the background somewhat.

The result was a good looking plot with a hard-to-read legend. I ended up placing a white rectangle just behind the legend, on top of my axis lines to hide them. What I didn't realize was that since my background was white, my rectagle wasn't vertically aligned well. A grey background highlights this well.

I played with the opacity too, and feedback from that went along the lines of "I like that one better, but I can't say why", so it's slightly more faded than the previous version.
